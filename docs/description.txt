## Introduction

This project explores cinema attendance patterns and ticket pricing across different federal states of Germany over time.  
The analysis aims to answer key questions regarding how cinema habits and infrastructure vary geographically and temporally,
and whether economic factors like ticket prices are correlated with audience behavior.

---

## Research Questions

1. **Evolution of Ticket Prices:**  
   How has the average cinema ticket price evolved over the years across different German states?

2. **Cinema Attendance Patterns:**  
   Which federal states have seen the highest and lowest number of cinema visits per inhabitant over time?

3. **Ticket Price vs. Attendance:**  
   Is there a relationship between average ticket prices and the number of cinema visits per inhabitant in each state?

4. **Infrastructure vs. Attendance:**  
   How does the number of cinemas and screens in a region relate to the number of cinema visitors?

5. **Population vs. Attendance:**  
   How is the number of residents in a region related to the number of cinema visitors?

---

## Suitable Analysis Methods

To investigate these questions, the following visual and statistical methods are applied:

- **Line Plot:**  
  To visualize the evolution of average ticket prices and cinema attendance over time for each federal state, highlighting both overall and regional trends.  
  (X-axis: Year, Y-axis: Price or Attendance, Lines: Federal States)

- **Bar Plot / Heatmap:**  
  To compare cinema visits per inhabitant across states and years, making it easy to spot regional differences and temporal patterns.

- **Scatter Plot:**  
  To explore relationships between:
  - Average ticket prices and cinema visits per inhabitant.
  - Number of cinemas/screens vs. number of cinema visitors.
  - Population size vs. cinema visits.
  
  These allow visual assessment of correlations and group distinctions (e.g., urban vs. rural).

- **Polynomial Trend Analysis:**  
  To model non-linear patterns in ticket prices and attendance that linear models may miss.

- **Spearman Correlation & Correlation Heatmaps:**  
  To quantify and visualize the strength and direction of monotonic relationships between key variables, useful when distributions are not normal.

- **Lollipop Chart:**  
  To summarize average annual percentage changes across main metrics, offering an accessible view of long-term industry trends.

---

## Python Packages / Custom Functions Used

- `pandas`: Data manipulation and preprocessing.
- `matplotlib.pyplot` and `seaborn`: For a wide variety of visualizations.
- `numpy`: Mathematical and statistical operations.
- `scipy.stats`: For correlation analysis, including Spearman's rank correlation.
- Custom plotting functions:
  - `plot_polynomial_trends`: Polynomial regression plots for trend analysis.
  - `compute_avg_annual_pct_change`: Calculates annual percentage change in key metrics.
  - `plot_price_attendance_over_time`: Visualizes relationship between price and attendance over time.
  - `print_spearman_correlation`: Computes and prints Spearman correlations for chosen variables.

By combining these techniques, this exploratory analysis provides detailed insights into how cinema infrastructure, pricing, and attendance have evolved and interacted over time across Germany.
